What I Do ?

  • User Research: Conducting user research to understand the target audience, their needs, and behaviors. This may involve surveys, interviews, usability testing, and analyzing user data.

  • Wireframing and Prototyping: Creating wireframes, which are basic layouts outlining the structure and functionality of a digital product. Prototyping involves building interactive models or mockups to test and validate design concepts.

  • Visual Design: Designing the visual elements of the interface, including color schemes, typography, icons, and imagery, to create an aesthetically pleasing and consistent user interface (UI).

  • Information Architecture: Defining the information structure and organization of the product, including navigation menus, content categorization, and hierarchy.

  • Interaction Design: Designing the interactions and behaviors of the product, including how users navigate, interact with elements, and accomplish tasks. This may involve creating microinteractions, animations, and transitions.

  • Usability Testing: Conducting user testing sessions to evaluate the usability and effectiveness of the design. Gathering feedback and iteratively refining the design based on user insights.

  • Collaboration: Working closely with cross-functional teams such as developers, product managers, and stakeholders to ensure the design aligns with the project goals, technical feasibility, and business requirements.
